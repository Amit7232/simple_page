A P J Abdul Kalam - Interesting Facts & Achievements on his 87th Birth Anniversary

FACT ABOUT APJ ABDUL KALAM

Modern Indian history is incomplete without the mention of APJ Abdul Kalam a scientist, teacher, statesman and above all a great human being. He is known as people's President and 'Missile Man of India'. He was born on 15 October 1931 on Pamban Island, Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. In 2015, UN declared his birthday as the World Student's Day. Here are 13 Interesting Facts about A P J Abdul Kalam on his 87th Birth Anniversary that you should not miss about him.

  • Jainulabudeen, Kalam’s father, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. His ancestors were wealthy but due to business failures, the family fortunes were lost and the former President was forced to do odd jobs. He used to sell newspapers to support his family.

  • Kalam missed an opportunity to become a fighter pilot for the Indian Air force. He was on the 9th spot on the list, and there were only 8 openings. The first 8 shortlisted candidates were recruited.

  • Kalam also made significant contributions to the nuclear capabilities of India. The Pokhran -2 nuclear tests (in 1998) were successful thanks to his organizational and technical support.

  • Kalam was frisked twice at JFK Airport in New York. He was checked for explosives by the airport staff. This incident was protested by India on the international level.

  • Kalam’s visit to Switzerland is commemorated as Science Day in the country. This declaration in his honor was made by the Swiss government after the sad demise of this great personality.

  • He was awarded the MTV Youth Icon award twice, in 2003 and 2006.

  • Once Kalam used Yahoo Answers to ask a question about terrorism. He asked, “What should we do to free our planet from terrorism?” He received more than 30,000 responses.

  • Kalam’s wish was to power the Rashtrapati Bhawan completely with solar power. However, this could not be implemented during the term of his presidency

  • Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam was conferred Padma Bhushan by Government of India in 1981

  • Later, in 1990, he received Padma Vibhushan Award for his immense contribution as scientific advisor to government and spearheading DRDO and ISRO.
